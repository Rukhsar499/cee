Mrida –The Pramanik Home Reimagined

Reimagining a blank canvas into a warm, rooted, and timeless home.

CATEGORY: Residential

YEAR: 2025

LOCATION: Kolkata, West Bengal

The Space

A 2,034 sq. ft. residence in Kolkata, spread across two levels, taken up for a full-scale renovation and interior transformation. The home offered generous volumes, large openings and a structurally sound framework yet it lacked identity, warmth and a cohesive spatial intent. While the bones were strong, the experience of the space felt unresolved and underdeveloped

The Existing Condition

At the time of takeover, the house felt expansive yet incomplete. Stark white walls and minimal furniture often pushed to the periphery created a sense of emptiness rather than openness. The interiors lacked layering, personality and visual engagement.

The staircase, finished in standard grey granite blended into the background. It was purely functional with no material or design articulation to contribute to the overall aesthetic of the home. Despite these, the home had a strong underlying advantage abundant natural light. Large openings allowed daylight to flood the interiors, offering a powerful foundation to build a more expressive and layered environment.

The Brief

The vision was to transform the residence into a home that feels traditional, warm, and deeply rooted, while remaining aligned with contemporary functionality and lifestyle needs.

The approach was guided by:

  • Re-engineering spatial planning across both levels
  • Introducing richness through materiality, texture, and detail
  • Enhancing usability through strategic civil and structural interventions
  • Creating strong focal elements to anchor the design narrative

Mrida was not a project driven by surface-level additions. It was about building character, depth and cohesion into a space that initially lacked all three.

The Transformation

Spatial Reconfiguration

The planning of both floors was carefully revisited to improve circulation, usability and spatial clarity. Instead of working around the existing layout, selective demolition allowed for a more resolved configuration.

Walls were dismantled and reconstructed where necessary, ensuring that each space felt intentional rather than leftover.

Before

After

A significant intervention took place in the kitchen. The existing common wall between the kitchen and living area was removed and reintroduced along a more strategic alignment connecting seamlessly with an existing passage opening. This shift not only improved functionality but also strengthened the spatial relationship between key areas of the home.

Flooring Continuity

One of the most impactful decisions was the complete replacement of flooring across both the ground and first floors

Rather than treating rooms as isolated units, a continuous flooring language was introduced throughout the home. This created a seamless visual flow, allowing spaces to transition effortlessly into one another while reinforcing a sense of unity.

The new flooring also played a key role in elevating the overall material palette acting as a neutral yet rich base upon which other elements could layer.

The Staircase – A Redefined Transition

Before

The staircase was finished in standard grey granite heavy, monotonous and visually disconnected from the rest of the home. It served its function but lacked any design intent or material warmth.

After

The staircase was completely reimagined as a central feature within the home:

  • Existing granite was fully dismantled

Carbonized bamboo wood flooring was introduced on the treads, bringing in warmth and texture 6” x 6” patterned tiles were used on the risers, adding a handcrafted, detailed character Edges were refined with subtle beading details, enhancing craftsmanship A polished finish elevated the overall aesthetic to a more premium level This transformation was further enriched by a terracotta-textured wall, which added depth and an earthy tonal balance

Before
After

A glass railing paired with a wooden handrail introduced visual lightness, ensuring the staircase remained open and connected to the surrounding space. What was once a purely transitional element is now a defining visual anchor within the home

The Kitchen

The existing kitchen was dull, boxy, and lacked character. Accessed through a small opening from the entrance passage, it felt restrictive and functionally disconnected.

The Process:

The space was completely reworked by dismantling the existing storage and removing the wall between the kitchen and living area. The original passage entry was closed, allowing for a more intuitive layout and improved circulation.

An open kitchen was introduced with a breakfast counter that connects seamlessly to the living space, detailed with Moroccan-patterned tiles for added warmth and character. Rebuilt entirely from scratch, the kitchen features pastel green cabinetry, white 15mm tiles and PU finished shutters creating a clean, functional and visually cohesive space The result is a space that feels open, efficient, and visually cohesive balancing everyday practicality with a refined design language.

The Process

The Mrida project involved extensive coordination across civil and interior scopes including dismantling existing structures, reconfiguring layouts, waterproofing, plumbing upgrades, complete flooring replacement and precision finishing.

Every stage was closely supervised to ensure that design intent translated seamlessly into execution while we handled the complexities on-site, allowing the client to experience the process without the usual stress.

The Outcome

Mrida stands close to our heart as it reflects our core approach transforming existing spaces into homes that feel new, functional, and deeply rooted.

The residence today is warm, cohesive, and layered with character. Natural light, material richness and thoughtful interventions come together to create a home that feels balanced, personal and timeless. What was once undefined has been reimagined into a space with identity and purpose where traditional sensibilities meet modern living.
